South Park

South Park is a TV show that has been around for decades now. It presents a lot of controversial topics in real life and uses its many different and unique characters to acheive this.

As visualized by the graphic, South Park has only 3 characters who speak more than 2% of the dialogue in the show. These three characters are Cartman (16%), Kyle (7.8%), and Stan (8.5%). This adds up to less than 33%. Less than a third of the show's dialogue comes from its 3 main characters.

This means that the rest of the 67% of the dialogue is spoken by many, many different characters who all play a small role. South Park has so many recognizable characters who appear at different times in the show to explore different themes.

This is what makes South Park so able to explore real life events. They have characters who represent all types of people and demographics in the world. As a satirical show this is a huge benefit because it allows them to create truly unique episodes.

In order for a TV show to successfuly air for 23 seasons the show must be able to explore a wide variety of topics with many characters. South Park has mastered this craft. Having dozens of characters who only speak around 1% of the show dialogue but still being recognizable is remarkable. This fact has allowed South Park to become the cultural phenomenon it is.
