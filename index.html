Data Driven Design

As an Information Scientist with a strong background in python and data analysis, I am always interested in finding data that leads to new solutions. I have translated this mindset to UX through my passion for Analytics and User Research. I have given a presentation to business offices at CU about data driven design which you can find here.

User Research

Arguably the most important part of the UX design process, I really enjoy talking to users and understanding their pain points. It's fascinating to me what you can discover in a few minutes from a user interview that you had no idea about.

Cognitive Science

Perhaps my favorite part of UX is that it combines psychology and technology. It is truly fascinating to me to design with cognitive biases in mind. My classes User Centered Design and Cognitive Science taught me so much about how users think and interact with interfaces.

Software Development

Although I don't consider myself a software developer, I have gained many skills in this area. I really enjoy the process of building something from scratch. I love the process of designing a website or concept and then coding into reality myself.

User Centered

As a UX designer I often feel like the stakeholder of the user in meetings. While other developers or project managers may be pushing for what is feasible or easy to build, I view it as my job to stick up for the user and push for what they want. A user centered design is always my focus.

Sketch/Invision

I have developed skills in many different design softwares. Most often I use Mural for lo-fidelity prototyping, Sketch for high-fidelity prototyping, and Invision to develop workable prototypes for user testing.
